Geographic Information Systems (GIS) are powerful tools that integrate spatial and geographic data to capture, store, analyze, manage, and present various types of information. By leveraging the spatial relationships among data points, GIS helps users visualize patterns, relationships, and trends. This capability makes GIS invaluable across numerous fields such as urban planning, environmental management, disaster response, and business analysis.

- **Definition and Basic Concepts**
  GIS (Geographic Information System) is a system for capturing, storing, analyzing, and managing spatial and geographic data.
- **Importance and Applications**
  GIS is used in various fields such as urban planning, environmental management, disaster response, and business analysis to visualize and interpret data patterns and relationships.
